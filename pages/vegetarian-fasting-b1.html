🥬 Vegetarian vs Fasting

Understanding the Difference - B1 Level

Hover over highlighted words for definitions and pronunciation

🌱 Vegetarian

Definition
Vegetarian /ˌvedʒɪˈteriən/ people do not eat meat /mit/. They eat plants /plænts/, eggs /egz/, and dairy /ˈderi/ products /ˈprɑdʌkts/ like milk and cheese.
Why People Choose This
People choose to be vegetarian to protect /prəˈtekt/ animals /ˈænəməlz/ and stay healthy /ˈhelθi/. It also helps the environment /ɪnˈvaɪrənmənt/.
Simple Example
It's like choosing to wear a comfortable /ˈkʌmftəbəl/ cotton T-shirt instead of real /riəl/ fur /fɜr/ because you love animals.

🧘 Fasting

Definition
Fasting /ˈfæstɪŋ/ means not eating meat AND also avoiding /əˈvɔɪdɪŋ/ strong /strɔŋ/ foods like onions /ˈʌnjənz/ and garlic /ˈgɑrlɪk/.
Why People Do This
People do this to keep their body /ˈbɑdi/ and mind /maɪnd/ pure /pjʊr/ and calm /kɑm/. It helps with meditation /ˌmedɪˈteɪʃən/ and spiritual /ˈspɪrɪtʃuəl/ practice /ˈpræktɪs/.
Simple Example
It's like being very quiet /ˈkwaɪət/ and focused /ˈfoʊkəst/ before an important test /test/ to prepare your mind.

🧪 Scientific Information

The five pungent spices /faɪv ˈpʌndʒənt ˈspaɪsəz/ (like scallions /ˈskæljənz/, garlic, chives /tʃaɪvz/, onions) contain natural /ˈnætʃərəl/ sulfur compounds /ˈsʌlfər ˈkɑmpaʊndz/.
