📜 般若波羅蜜多心經

中文譯本：大唐三藏法師 玄奘（602–664）
英文譯本：James Legge（1815–1897）
B1 學習英譯：本站協同 ChatGPT 製作，供初學者參考

🙏 開經偈

無上甚深微妙法，百千萬劫難遭遇。我今見聞得受持，願解如來真實義。

Homage to the Supreme Dharma: profound and subtle, rare to encounter through eons.
Now that I see, hear, and receive it, may I understand the Tathāgata’s true intent.

📖 經文對照

觀自在菩薩，行深般若波羅蜜多時，照見五蘊皆空，度一切苦厄。

Legge: Avalokiteshvara, while practicing deeply the Prajnaparamita, clearly saw that the five aggregates are all empty, and so relieved all suffering.

B1: Avalokiteshvara saw that everything is empty, and it helped him overcome suffering.

舍利子，色不異空，空不異色；色即是空，空即是色；受想行識，亦復如是。

Legge: Form is not different from emptiness, and emptiness is not different from form. Form is emptiness; emptiness is form. So too are feeling, perception, volition, and consciousness.

B1: Form is the same as emptiness. Feeling and thought are the same too.

舍利子，是諸法空相，不生不滅，不垢不淨，不增不減。

Legge: All dharmas are marked with emptiness: not born, not destroyed, not impure, not pure, neither increasing nor decreasing.

B1: All things are empty. Nothing is born or ends, nothing is dirty or clean.

是故空中無色，無受想行識，無眼耳鼻舌身意，無色聲香味觸法，

Legge: Therefore in emptiness there is no form, no feeling, no perception, no volition, no consciousness,

B1: In emptiness there are no senses, no thoughts, no mind.

無眼界，乃至無意識界，無無明，亦無無明盡，乃至無老死，亦無老死盡。

Legge: No eye, ear, nose, tongue, body, mind; no form, sound, smell, taste, touch, or dharmas; no realm of eyes and so forth until no realm of mind consciousness; no ignorance and also no extinction of ignorance; and so forth until no old age and death and also no extinction of old age and death.

B1: No eye, ear, nose, tongue, body, or mind; no sights, sounds, smells, tastes, touches, or things; no world of eyes or consciousness; no ignorance or end of ignorance; no aging or death, or end of aging or death.

無苦集滅道，無智亦無得，以無所得故。

Legge: There is no suffering, no origin, no cessation, no path. There is no wisdom and no gain. Because nothing is gained.

B1: No suffering, no cause, no end, no path. No wisdom or gains. Nothing to get.

菩提薩埵，依般若波羅蜜多故，心無罣礙；無罣礙故，無有恐怖，

Legge: Because bodhisattvas rely on Prajnaparamita, their minds have no hindrance; because there is no hindrance, they have no fear.

B1: Bodhisattvas rely on wisdom, so their minds have no obstacles and no fear.

遠離顛倒夢想，究竟涅槃。三世諸佛，依般若波羅蜜多故，得阿耨多羅三藐三菩提。

Legge: They are far from delusions and reach Nirvana. The Buddhas of the three times rely on Prajnaparamita and attain unsurpassed perfect enlightenment.

B1: They leave illusions behind and reach Nirvana. Buddhas of past, present, and future rely on this wisdom to become fully awakened.

故知般若波羅蜜多是大神咒，是大明咒，是無上咒，是無等等咒，

Legge: Therefore, know that Prajnaparamita is the great mantra, the bright mantra, the supreme mantra, the unequalled mantra,

B1: So know that this wisdom is the great mantra, the bright mantra, the best mantra, the only mantra.

能除一切苦，真實不虛。故說般若波羅蜜多咒，即說咒曰：

Legge: Which removes all suffering, true and not false. Thus is the Prajnaparamita mantra proclaimed:

B1: It removes all pain, it is true. So we say the wisdom mantra:

揭諦 揭諦 波羅揭諦 波羅僧揭諦 菩提薩婆訶。

Legge: Gate gate paragate parasamgate bodhi svaha.

B1: Go! Go! Go beyond! Go fully beyond! Enlightenment! Hurray!
